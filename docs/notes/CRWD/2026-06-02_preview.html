Setup

CrowdStrike enters its Q1 FY2027 print off a strong Q4 FY2026: total revenue of $1.31 billion (+23% YoY), ending ARR of $5.25 billion (+24% YoY), and the largest net-new ARR quarter reported in the trailing year ($330.7 million). Non-GAAP subscription gross margin expanded to 81% and non-GAAP operating income grew 45% YoY -- profitability scaling faster than revenue. The market's key question heading into this print is whether that Q4 acceleration was a one-quarter pop or the start of a sustained recovery in net-new business following the well-documented July 19 incident that pressured results through much of FY2026.

  • Non-GAAP EPS — we call BEAT vs. guided $1.06–$1.07. Margin expansion in Q4 (subscription GM 81%, op income +45% YoY) suggests operating leverage that should persist into Q1.

This is a historically tight, conservative band; we don't have a specific reason to expect a material beat or miss on the ARR balance itself, though net-new ARR dollars (not a separately guided line) is the figure we'll watch closest on print day.

What matters

  • Does net-new ARR ($330.7M in Q4) continue to reaccelerate, or was Q4 a one-quarter pop against an easy prior-year compare?
  • Non-GAAP operating margin trend -- is Q4's leverage gain durable, or partly a one-time cost timing benefit?
  • Any commentary on lingering July-19-incident-related costs/recoveries in the non-GAAP reconciliation language -- these have been explicitly called out as a non-GAAP add-back in recent releases and are worth tracking until fully lapped.
