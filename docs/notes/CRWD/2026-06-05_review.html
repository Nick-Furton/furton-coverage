What the 10-Q added

The 10-Q (filed 2026-06-04, one day after the 8-K) shows total deferred revenue -- current ($3,370.2M) plus noncurrent ($1,352.0M) -- of about $4,722.2M as of April 30, 2026, versus $4,753.4M at the prior balance-sheet date (January 31, 2026) implied by the same line items' comparative column. That is a modest sequential decline in total deferred revenue even as ARR and revenue both grew -- a nuance the 8-K's ARR/revenue headline doesn't surface on its own, and worth watching alongside the net-new-ARR softness flagged in the flash: it's consistent with either a timing mix shift in contract billing/prepayment schedules, or a genuine, if modest, softening in forward-booked business. Thesis update

The core beat-and-raise pattern is intact and the 10-Q doesn't contradict the 8-K's headline strength, but two things temper unqualified optimism heading into Q2 FY2027: net-new ARR dipped sequentially, and total deferred revenue also declined modestly quarter over quarter. Management's own full-year net-new-ARR guidance raise implies they expect both to reaccelerate later in FY2027 rather than reflecting a genuine deceleration -- that is now our tracked forward call (see calls: above) to grade against the Q2 FY2027 print.
