## Guidance changes

CrowdStrike guided Q2 FY2027 revenue of $1,436.0M–$1,442.0M and non-GAAP EPS of
$1.16–$1.17, and **raised** full-year FY2027 non-GAAP EPS guidance to $4.88–$4.96
(from $4.78–$4.90 given back in March). The release's own headline explicitly calls
out "raises FY27 net new ARR growth guidance by 520 basis points at the midpoint."
Note: all of these guidance figures are stated on a **pre-split** basis -- the same
release also announces a four-for-one stock split (record date 2026-06-25, effective
2026-07-01, trading split-adjusted from 2026-07-02), which matters for any future
comparison against post-split per-share figures.

## First-read verdict

A clean beat-and-raise print on the headline financials: revenue and non-GAAP EPS both
came in above the guided range, and forward guidance was raised rather than merely
reaffirmed. The one place our preview's thesis did NOT hold up is net-new ARR: we
called a continuation of Q4 FY26's reacceleration ($330.7M net new ARR), but Q1 FY27
added $255.8M -- down sequentially, even though total ARR still grew 24% year-over-year
to roughly $5.51 billion. The company's own framing (raising full-year net-new-ARR
growth guidance) suggests management reads the underlying trend as intact despite the
sequential dip, but our specific qualitative call was too optimistic about Q1
specifically. This is exactly the kind of miss the scorecard exists to surface honestly.
